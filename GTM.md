# GTM (Go-to-market)

## Target user
- Seed to Series B founders / operators at startups spending meaningful money on AI tools and API usage.
- Teams with 2–50 seats on coding assistants + multiple LLM subscriptions.
- Common pain: “We’re bleeding money across tools and don’t know what to cut without slowing engineering.”

## Core value prop
- A fast, credible audit that identifies plan waste and suggests optimizations.
- A clear savings number and action plan.
- Option to explore discounted AI credits via Credex for high spend.

## Acquisition channels (specific)
1. Founder communities where spend concerns are real:
   - YC Founder Slack / Bookface (if accessible)
   - r/startups and r/SaaS (target threads about AI costs)
   - Indie Hackers ("tool stack" and "costs" posts)
2. Developer/AI engineering communities:
   - Cursor/Copilot/Claude/GPT subreddit threads about pricing
   - Discords for AI tooling
3. Direct outbound:
   - Search LinkedIn for “Head of Engineering” at startups hiring ML/AI roles, send a short note with a 1-minute audit claim.

## First 100 users plan
- Publish 10 example “sanitized audits” as share links (no company info) to demonstrate value.
- Offer a "Founding cohort" free consult for teams with >$500/mo savings.
- Post 5 short build-in-public updates with screenshots and a clear CTA.
- Ask every user who submits email to forward the share link internally.

## Viral loop
- The product creates a shareable report URL with OG previews.
- Encourage users to share a sanitized result with their team or on social.

## Positioning
- “Mint for AI subscriptions” + “AI tooling bill analyzer”.
- Emphasize credibility: rule-based engine + citations for pricing sources.
