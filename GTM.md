# GTM (Go-to-market)

## Exact target user (who this is *for*)
**Primary buyer/user:** *Head of Engineering / CTO* at a **Seed to Series A** SaaS company (roughly **10–80 employees**) where:
- Engineering is **5–30 people**
- They have started paying for **Copilot/Cursor/ChatGPT/Claude** seats
- Someone is also running meaningful **OpenAI/Anthropic/Gemini API** spend

**Why this persona now:** This person is accountable for engineering velocity *and* burn. They feel the “AI tooling bill creep” but don’t have time to audit plans, seats, and overlapping tools. They need a quick answer to: *“What can we cut this week without slowing the team?”*

## Intent triggers: what they Google / scroll right before needing it
Right before wanting this tool, they’re usually doing one of these:
- Reviewing the month-end credit card statement or AWS bill and thinking: *“Why did AI spend jump 2x?”*
- Preparing for a board/investor update and trying to show cost control
- Being asked by finance: *“Can you justify these subscriptions?”*

What they search or read:
- Google: “Cursor vs Copilot pricing”, “ChatGPT Team vs Plus cost”, “Claude Team pricing”, “OpenAI API credits discount”, “reduce AI tooling spend”, “LLM API cost optimization”, “per-seat AI tools cost for engineering team”
- Reddit threads like: “Is Copilot Business worth it?”, “Cursor pricing is insane”, “Do we need both Claude and ChatGPT?”
- Hacker News comments on posts about AI coding tools and subscription fatigue

## Where they hang out online (specific, real places)
- **Hacker News** (threads on AI tooling, startup ops, and cost discipline)
- **r/startups**, **r/SaaS**, **r/Entrepreneur** (cost-cutting and tool stack posts)
- **r/ChatGPT**, **r/ClaudeAI**, **r/OpenAI**, **r/GitHubCopilot**, **r/cursor** (pricing/plan comparisons)
- **Indie Hackers** (threads: “tool stack”, “monthly expenses”, “what are you paying for AI?”)
- **LinkedIn**: CTO/VP Eng/Head of Eng feeds; CTO circles discussing spend governance

(If available through the Credex network: private founder/CTO Slack groups where operators share vendor spend pain.)

## First 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget (specific plan)
**Week 1 (seed content + distribution hooks):**
1. Create **10 “example audits”** as share links using realistic but anonymized inputs (e.g., “15 engineers, $1,900/mo AI spend, $620/mo savings”).
2. Post **1 actionable teardown per day** (7 posts) with a screenshot + share link:
   - 2 on Indie Hackers
   - 2 on r/SaaS / r/startups (in existing “expenses/tool stack” threads, not spam)
   - 2 as LinkedIn posts aimed at CTOs (show the before/after cost breakdown)
   - 1 as a Hacker News “Show HN” (focused on: deterministic rules + citations)

**Week 2 (direct outreach that doesn’t feel like sales):**
3. Build a list of **120 Seed–A CTO/Heads of Eng** (public info): companies hiring “AI Engineer”, “LLM”, “Applied AI” roles are strong signals of spend.
4. Send **personalized messages**:
   - Subject: “Quick AI spend audit? (2 minutes, deterministic)"
   - Include: 1 screenshot + “If you paste your tool spends, you’ll get a shareable report link for your finance team.”
   - Target: **10–15 messages/day**.

**Week 3 (viral loop):**
5. Add a simple ask in the product: “Forward this share link to whoever owns the company card / finance.”
6. Offer a **free 15-min consult** only when the audit detects **>$500/mo savings** (keeps it scarce and relevant).

**Week 4 (community proof):**
7. Publish a “Top 10 AI tooling waste patterns we saw this month” post (based on anonymized aggregates).

## The unfair distribution channel (what only Credex/you can do)
**Credex unfair advantage:** if Credex can actually offer **discounted API credits** or negotiated pricing, the product becomes more than a “calculator.” The audit can end with a concrete next step: *“We can reduce your API effective rate by X% — request access.”* That’s a strong reason to share and to submit an email.

Additionally, Credex can leverage:
- Existing relationships with startups already buying credits
- A higher-trust intro path (“we can directly help lower the bill,” not just advise)

## What week-1 traction looks like (if it’s working)
In the first 7 days, success looks like:
- **300–600 landing page visits** from posts + outbound
- **60–120 audits started**
- **30–60 completed audits** (50% completion from started)
- **15–30 share links created** (because sharing is the “report” moment)
- **10–20 leads captured** (email submissions), with **3–5** requesting a follow-up because savings are large

If those numbers show up, week 2 focus is scaling the best channel (likely outbound + LinkedIn) and tightening the share/lead funnel copy.
